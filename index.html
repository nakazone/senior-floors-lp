Frequently Asked Questions — Hardwood Floor Refinishing Denver

How long does hardwood floor refinishing take?

Most residential projects in Denver take 2–3 days from start to finish. This includes sanding, staining (if desired), and 3 coats of finish with drying time between each coat. We'll give you a precise timeline during your free in-home estimate.

Do I need to leave my home during refinishing?

We recommend staying out of the rooms being refinished during the process and for 24 hours after the final coat. For whole-home projects, many customers stay with family or in a hotel for 2–3 nights. We use low-VOC finishes to minimize fumes.

How much does hardwood floor refinishing cost in Denver?

Most Denver homeowners pay between $3–$6 per square foot for professional sanding and refinishing. A 500 sq ft living room typically runs $1,500–$3,000. Factors that affect price include floor condition, stain color choice, number of rooms, and finish type. We provide a detailed, no-obligation quote during your free in-home estimate.

Can my floors be refinished, or do they need to be replaced?

Most solid hardwood floors can be refinished 5–8 times over their lifetime. Engineered hardwood can typically be refinished 1–3 times depending on the veneer thickness. During our free estimate, we'll inspect your floors and give you an honest assessment — we'll never recommend refinishing if replacement is truly needed.

What's the difference between sanding and buffing / screening?

Full sanding removes the top layer of wood to eliminate deep scratches, stains, and wear. Buffing (or screening) is a lighter process that scuffs the existing finish so a new coat can adhere — it's faster and cheaper but only works on lightly worn floors. We'll recommend the right option based on your floor's condition.

Do you offer dustless sanding?

Yes. We use dustless sanding equipment that captures 98%+ of dust at the source. This means less cleanup, no dust coating your furniture, and a healthier environment during and after the project.
